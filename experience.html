NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory Education Intern (2022 - Present)

Division 32 (Planetary Science)

In August 2022, I began my first internship at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory in La Canada Flintridge, California. I was assigned to their Planetary Science Division to work under Dr. Robert Anderson and Dr. Keith Chin as my primary mentors.

Originally starting in a team of two co-interns, I focused on the creation of new Python software interfaces for our data acquisition instruments and various motor controlled hardware. Additionally, I took the lead on collecting and processing data from our lunar and martian soil samples including cone penetration strain, vane shear strength, and ice/water detection.

(SPARTA) Soil Properties Assessment Resistance and Thermal Analysis

SPARTA is an automated probe designed to test soil penetration strength, shear strength, and water/ice content. Its hardware consists of a load cell, a torque sensor, a dielectric spectrometer, an RTD, and an inertial measurement unit.

My work so far has consisted of the following:

  • Designed and interfaced a program using sensor instruments (i.e. load cells, torque sensors, dielectric spectrometers) within a Python Tkinter GUI to coincide with the construction a new SPARTA test bench for soil property data acquisition/analysis.
  • Replaced previous Linux system provided by Honeybee Robotics with Windows based software, integrating all bench components into one unified platform to maximize operating clarity and data acquisition efficiency
    • Enabled simultaneous live plot monitoring and direct user control of each bench component
    • Streamlined data processing by replacing the need for manual data-type conversions across Windows and Linux
  • Expected to deploy a nearly finalized and fully automated XML and Python variant of the test bench software within a zero-gravity Blue Origin flight payload in June 2024
  • Conducted test bench field experiments using Lunar and Martian soil samples
  • Collaborated on a paper with mentors by providing data plots and experiment notes (i.e. soil strain, shear strength, water/ice content)

CSUN - Office of Faculty Affairs - Student Assistant (2019 - 2023)

Starting in my first week of college at California State University Northridge, I was able to secure my first part time job as an office student assistant in the Department of Faculty Affairs.

My job mainly consisted of:

  • Answering faculty related questions on the phone
  • Assisting management and organization of form databases (Interfolio, Onbase)
  • Updating webpages on the Faculty Affairs webpage
  • Redirecting calls and emails to the appropriate employee and/or department
  • Managing the main Faculty Affairs inbox account
  • Helping organizing faculty related events (making calendars, setting up conferences, etc.)
  • Delivering gifts and mail around campus

Faculty Affairs gave me a wonderful and convenient place to work at every week while I attended school. With me being initially very shy, it provided me with a great starting point to break out of my social comfort zone. Whether it be answering a phone, relaying information to my supervisors, or delivering mail, I gradually learned how to profesionally communicate in a workplace environment.

The job also showed me how much important work goes into the backend of faculty hiring, public relations, grievance/issue resolution, document record keeping, and so much more.

Additionally, having the workplace building be on campus allowed me to work during my down time in between classes, and also have an exclusive place for privacy and quiet whenever I needed it (i.e. attending an online class, or taking a test).
