Academics & Work Experience

NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory Education Intern (2022 - Present)


Division 32 (Planetary Science)

In August 2022, I began working as an intern at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, California. I was assigned to their Planetary Science Division to work under Dr. Robert Anderson and Dr. Keith Chin as my primary mentors.


(SPARTA) Soil Properties Assessment Resistance and Thermal Analysis

SPARTA is an automated probe designed to test soil penetration strength, shear strength, and water/ice content. Its hardware consists of a load cell, a torque sensor, a di-electric spectrometer, an thermal couple, and an inertial measurement unit.

My project assignment focused on data acquisition instrumentation, and software interfacing.

In regards to software, I used Python to create a guided user interface from scratch to account for the new experimental SPARTA test benches using new parts. Along with the user interfacing, I also had to work with serial port communication in order to operate the hardware through a combination of Python and an Arduino board.

In addition to working with serial ports, I got to learn how to work with power supplies and a fair share of electrical wiring to assist my Mechanical Engineering co-intern. This included calibration of voltage in data acquisition devices, connecting relays and an Arduino board to a linear actuator, and connecting a motor controller to a torque rotary motor.
