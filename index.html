What Is This Site For?

This website's main purpose is to showcase my academic and related work/interning experiences, my web engineering skills, and the fruits of some of my personal hobbies!

Brief Introduction

I received my Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science at California State University Northridge in Fall 2023. Though I have my diploma, I'm still waiting to attend my official graduation ceremony in May of 2024.

As of right now, I am still working as a part time intern for NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. I'm sort of on "standby" while also actively looking for full-time work.

In my free time outside of academics, I love to play and cover my favorite songs on drums, draw, and create cosplays (i.e. costumes) of my favorite characters from anime and other pop-culture media!

Why Computer Science?

If you're interested to know how I became invested in Computer Science, I actually started programming in Java when I was about 9-10 years old. Minecraft was a video game I was pretty addicted to back then, so when I was curious to learn how to make mods and changes to the game, I found out about Java and the massive online community for modding the game.



I published my own mods on MinecraftForum.com (which are still up today) and received somewhere around 20,000 total downloads. Even though I didn't really know what I was doing on a more technical level since I mostly followed programming tutorials on a language I had not studied at all, boy was it fun! I remember posting about all of my syntax errors on a forum asking for help, and how many people I probably pissed off for not knowing what anything in the compiler meant.

I got to add new items, monsters, and mechanics to the base game with anything my 10-year-old brain could come up with. Not only that, it also gave me more time to spend with my brother, as he did lots of the pixel art for the new items I added to the game.
